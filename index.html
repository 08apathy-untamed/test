✦ Did You Know

Ducks are scientifically
incredible.

🧴

Natural Waterproofing

Ducks produce a special oil from a gland near their tail. They spread it across their feathers, creating a waterproof coating that keeps them dry even during a rainstorm.

💤

Half-Brain Sleep

Ducks can sleep with one eye open — literally. They practice unihemispheric sleep, keeping half their brain awake to watch for predators while the other half rests.

🗺️

Epic Migrations

Some duck species fly thousands of miles between seasons. The Garganey, for instance, migrates from Europe all the way to sub-Saharan Africa each year.

🦷

Bill Filters

Duck bills aren't just cute — they're precision tools. Lamellae (tiny comb-like structures) inside the bill filter out food from water with remarkable efficiency.

🧲

Magnetic Navigation

Ducks have an internal compass. They can detect the Earth's magnetic field, helping them navigate during migration even in complete darkness or overcast skies.

🐣

Instant Imprinting

Ducklings imprint on the first moving thing they see after hatching. This is usually their mother — but sometimes it's a human, a dog, or even a toy boat.

✦ The Case For Ducks

Reasons ducks deserve your love

01

The Waddle

Few things in nature are as purely joyful as watching a duck waddle with absolute conviction. They own that walk. Zero apologies.

02

The Quack

Universally recognized. Endlessly satisfying. A mallard's quack is the sonic equivalent of a warm hug. Science probably agrees.

03

They Thrive Everywhere

City parks, remote Arctic tundra, tropical rainforests — ducks adapt and flourish. They are the world travelers of the bird kingdom.

04

The Upending Dive

A duck feeding upside-down in a pond, tail in the air, is peak comedy. They do this constantly and with total seriousness.

05

Duckling Parades

A mother duck leading a line of tiny ducklings is one of the most heartwarming spectacles nature has to offer. Undeniable.

06

Pure Vibes

Ducks radiate calm. Floating serenely on a pond while paddling furiously underneath — there's a life lesson in there somewhere.
